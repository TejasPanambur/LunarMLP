A Foundational Multimodal Model for Lunar Surface Correlation Discovery: Uncovering Hydration and Beyond

A Foundational Model for Lunar Science

CVPR 2025

Our work, developed at NASA's Frontier Development Lab, introduces a pioneering self-supervised, massively multimodal masked model (4M). It analyzes multimodal lunar data from the Moon Mineralogy Mapper (M³) to uncover complex correlations between surface hydration, mineralogy, and morphology, paving the way for future lunar resource utilization.

Cracking the Moon's Daily OH/H₂O Cycle

Why Lunar Hydration Matters for the Future of Space Exploration

🌙 The Mystery of Lunar Water

Since 2009, M³ has revealed that the Moon hosts a complex OH/H₂O cycle, despite its airless, arid regolith. The 2.7–3 μm absorption band is a direct proxy for hydroxyl (OH) and water (H₂O) on lunar soils.

The Daily Pattern: That band is deep at sunrise, diminishes at midday, then deepens again in the afternoon. The amplitude and constant component vary with location.

Why it matters: Pinpointing these drivers is critical for future in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) and for interpreting upcoming data from Lunar Trailblazer.

Initial Insights from M³ Data

"Where Minerals Meet Water: Early Correlation Discovery"

During the initial phases of data collection and analysis, we identified interesting relationships between mineralogical data and hydration signatures.

Key Correlations Discovered:

  • Morning Hydration ↔ Pyroxene: Regions rich in pyroxene have a higher hydration signature, especially during the early morning hours
  • Titanium ↔ Midday Dehydration: Regions with high titanium abundance have a lower hydration signature during midday

🔬 The Challenge

Manual analysis of large planetary datasets becomes laborious, and the human eye might even overlook some relationships. We needed an automated approach to discover complex, non-linear correlations.

🤖 Our Solution

We developed the first lunar foundation model using the Massively Multimodal Masked Model (4M) framework - a transformer-based approach that seamlessly integrates multimodal lunar data.

🎯 What We've Achieved

Built a Lunar Foundation Model validating key hydration links: Strong Pyroxene ↔ IBD₃μm correlation confirmed, and weaker but notable Olivine ↔ IBD₃μm relationship observed.

Our Lunar Multimodal ML Pipeline

"From Pixels to Predictions: The ABCs of Multimodal Training"

🚀 The Revolutionary A-B-C Approach

We developed a novel three-stage pipeline that transforms diverse lunar datasets into unified multimodal representations:

  1. A - Abstraction: Tokenize Lunar Modalities
    Each data source (spectral, mineralogical, elemental, topographic) is equipped with its own fully trained tokenizer. Every 128×128 patch is run through its modality's VQ encoder to generate discrete token IDs from unique codebooks.
  2. B - Bridging: Masked Autoencoder Learns Cross-Modal Representations
    Once the tokenizers are trained, we train a transformer encoder-decoder to learn cross-modal relationships. Training uses a Dirichlet distribution to randomly select input tokens from different modalities.
  3. C - Causal Correlation Discovery
    The trained multimodal transformer can predict any modality from any other modality, enabling us to discover which modalities drive hydration signatures across the lunar surface.

🔬 Technical Innovation

Tokenization Architecture: Novel spatial and spectral encoding specifically tailored to hyperspectral data, capturing both spatial structure and spectral signatures across 85+ channels.

Masking Strategy: Uses Dirichlet distribution to encourage cross-modal learning rather than simple within-modality reconstruction.

Foundation Model: First application of the 4M framework to planetary hyperspectral data, enabling unified analysis of diverse lunar datasets.

Tokenizer Architecture Deep Dive

Image tokenizer architecture based on DiVAE (Shi et al., 2022).

🧠 A Multi-Modal Tokenization Strategy

Our novel approach leverages a suite of specialized tokenizers to handle the unique challenges of diverse lunar data. We process each modality—from complex hyperspectral cubes to simple text—with a dedicated tokenizer before fusing them into a unified model.

Hyperspectral Tokenizer: Captures both spatial features (patches of the lunar surface) and spectral signatures (data across different wavelengths) from 3D data cubes.

Image Tokenizer: Efficiently encodes standard 2D images, such as mineral maps, into compact representations using a DiVAE-based architecture.

Text Tokenizer: Converts tabular data, such as latitude, longitude, and time of day, into meaningful embeddings for the model.

This multi-pronged tokenization ensures that every piece of information, regardless of its original format, is processed effectively before being integrated into the main model.

Causal Correlation Discovery

"Testing Which Modalities Drive Hydration & Mineral Relationships"

🔍 Cross-Modal Inference Process

For this work, all experiments and validation were conducted solely among three modalities: Pyroxene, Olivine, and Morning IBD₃μm (hydration).

Our inference pipeline enables us to:

  • Sanity Check: Confirm the model can reconstruct one modality accurately before testing causal links
  • Within-Modality Fidelity: Test reconstruction quality - tokenizer-only vs multimodal fusion
  • Cross-Modal Inference: Discover which modalities correlate with hydration patterns

Key Validation Results:

Tokenizer-Only: Rebuilds pyroxene maps with minimal blur and fine textures

Multimodal Fusion: Introduces extra smoothing around edges but retains overall footprint

Takeaway: Cross-modal errors in bridging phase reflect causal complexity, not broken autoencoder

Groundbreaking Results

Validating core mineral-hydration correlations and pushing the boundaries of model performance.

🔍 Validated Core Modal Correlations

Our model successfully confirmed and quantified the relationship between key minerals and lunar hydration signatures, revealing critical causal links.

🟢 Pyroxene ↔ IBD₃μm (Hydration)

Strong, Qualitatively Significant Link Confirmed: Our findings show that pyroxene abundance is the strongest causal predictor of morning hydration signatures. This suggests pyroxene-rich regions are key targets for future water resource exploration.

🟡 Olivine ↔ IBD₃μm (Hydration)

Weaker but Notable Relationship Observed: Olivine shows a measurable, though less predictive, correlation with hydration, adding another layer of understanding to the lunar water cycle.

🎯 Reconstruction Quality & Fidelity

Before testing causal links, we validated our model's ability to reconstruct what it sees. The high fidelity of our diffusion tokenizer ensures that any cross-modal inference errors reflect true causal complexity, not a broken autoencoder.

Tokenizer-Only Performance: Rebuilds mineral maps with minimal blur and preserves fine textures.

Key Insight: This high-fidelity reconstruction provides a reliable baseline for discovering genuine mineral-water relationships.

🧪 Scientific Implications

These findings suggest that pyroxene-rich regions on the Moon are prime locations for retaining water/hydroxyl signatures. This could be due to unique thermal properties, surface roughness affecting solar wind implantation, or mineralogical composition influencing water formation. This work provides a critical roadmap for future ISRU missions and instruments like the Lunar Trailblazer.
